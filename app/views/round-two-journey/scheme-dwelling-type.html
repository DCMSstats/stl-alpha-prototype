Check the type of dwelling that needs registration

All short-term let accommodation in England needs registration. This is unless it's one of the excluded types of accommodation. The short-term let registration scheme covers residential and commercial accommodation in England, including:

  • Home Sharing: Renting out part or all of your home while you are living there.
  • Home Letting: Renting out part or all of your home while you are not living there, such as during a holiday. Including a single room in a primary residence, a self- contained part of a primary residence (e.g. a basement with kitchen and bathroom) or converted garage.
  • Secondary Letting: Renting out a property where you do not normally live, such as a second home or holiday let, including letting out a single room or multiple rooms.
  • Combined Home Letting and Home Sharing: Renting out part or all of your home both while you are living there and when you are not, including single rooms in a property where all rooms are to let short-term.
  • Other properties: including treehouses, gatehouses, farmhouses on farms' grounds. Including renting out part or all of the property, both while you are living there and when or if you are not.
