Apply for a short-term let registration: step by step

Find out what you need to do to register your short-term let in England.

For your security, your application will be reset if you are inactive for more than two hours. If you need to stop at any point, save the application and return to it later.

You can leave this application at any point and your progress will be saved. You can return to this application at any time until 19 September 2024 at 16:59.

  1. Step 1: Complete the declaration

    By continuing this application you agree that the information you will give is complete and correct.

  2. Step 2: Fill in your personal details

    1. Enter details about the registered owner.

      Details will include the owner name, date of birth, contact details and addresses.

  3. Step 3: Add short-term let property addresses

    Add one or multiple properties. Fill in their details.

  4. Step 4: Fill in property details

    1. Add property details to addresses added.

  5. and Upload documents

    You will be asked to upload a gas safety certificate for your properties (if applicable).

    You will also be asked to confirm your property meets the necessary safety requirements for fire and electrical safety.

  6. Step 5: Pay for your application(s)

    Each application costs £45 per property. You can pay for a single or multiple short-term let properties.

    You can pay by MasterCard, Visa, Electron or Delta debit or credit card. Use GOV.UK to pay for your application.

  7. Step 6: Submit application

    Your application will be reviewed and a decision will be made within approximately 5 weeks. You will be notified of this via email.
